Education: I am a Ph.D. student in machine learning working with Marco Cuturi (Apple MLR). Prior to that, I completed a master’s degree in Mathematics, Vision and Learning (MVA) at ENS Paris-Saclay. During my master’s thesis, I worked with Claire Boyer (Sorbonne Université), Julie Josse (INRIA) and Boris Muzellec (Owkin) at LPSM.

Research: I study the interplay between optimal transport (OT) and machine learning. My primary goal is to demonstrate how OT can be used to introduce inductive biases in various learning contexts, particularly in generative modeling and representational learning. For instance, my work has been applied to unpaired image translation, disentangled representation learning, and various single-cell genomics problems like trajectory inference, perturbation effect prediction, and modality translation. I am especially interested in these life sciences applications. This interest led me to join Fabian Theis’ lab at Helmholtz Munich as a visting Ph.D. student in February 2024, where I have been working on generative models for single-cell perturbation discovery. Additionally, I interned at the Flatiron Institute from June to August 2024, collaborating with Victor Chardes and Surya Maddu in Michael Shelley’s team to develop generative models for inferring biophysical dynamics from omics data. Currently, I am focused on using OT to enhance the training and performance of foundation models for computational biology and language modeling, with a specific interest in state-space models. During fall 2024, I will be interning at Amazon AI with Matthew Tragger, within the fundamental research team led by Alessandro Achille, where I will explore these ideas further.
